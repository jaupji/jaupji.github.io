GRASSLANDS

Hello, person!

Are you here to learn about the grasslands biome? If yes then this is the right page!

It is believed that there are at least 80 species of animals that live in the grassland biome. But the main animals and plants are giraffes, zebras, buffaloes, kangaroos, mice, moles, gophers, ground squirrels, snakes, worms, termites, beetles, lions, leopards, hyenas, and elephants. The various species of grasses include purple needlegrass, blue grama, buffalo grass, and galleta. Flowers include asters, blazing stars, coneflowers, goldenrods, sunflowers, clovers, psoraleas, and wild indigos. The average temperature in the grassland is -20°C to 30°C. What does climate change do to grasslands? Increasingly severe and frequent droughts, floods, fires, and hurricanes are likely to affect U.S. grassland ecosystems. Drought exacerbates soil erosion and aquifer depletion. Greater variability in precipitation will favor more frequent fires, which can reduce encroachment of woody plants into grasslands.
